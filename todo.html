EXPLORE

Benne on Eagle

Benne on Eagle is an upscale black-owned restaurant in the historic Eagle Street neighborhood. They specialize in seasonal menus focused on African-inspired sharing plates & a warm atmosphere. Benne on Eagle welcomes all – from those who grew up in and around The Block, to folks who might be visiting our town for the first time. We invite you to join us in bringing re-invigorated life and soul back to The Block and enjoying some great food while you’re at it.
Cost: ~$95 dinner for two Age: M for mature (recommended)



Asheville Art Museum

Located in the heart of downtown Asheville, the Art museum boasts a wide range of interactive programs, artwork and sculptures from the talented local artists, and American art from all across the US. The Collection also preserves the artistic traditions of the Southern Appalachian region, reflects the unique combination of cultures in Western North Carolina, and explores the mutual influence of Southern Appalachia on American art.
Cost: Free-$5 donation Age: E for Everyone



Mayfels' Restaurant

Mayfels restaurant is known for its cool patio seating, cajun style cooking, and affordable drink prices. Mayfel’s is located at 22 College Street, in the heart of downtown Asheville, WNC. In addition to our dining room, we also offer year-round, dog-friendly seating on our covered patio, which looks out onto historic Pritchard Park, home to the famous Friday night drum circle. We also have a seasonal courtyard bar, which is like a little slice of the French Quarter right here in the mountains.
Cost:~$50 for two Age:21+ (recommended)



Asheville Hiking

Asheville being located in the mountains, has breath-taking scenery, rich with natural waterfalls, lush forests, and beautiful local wildlife. While exploring on your own is always welcome, a guided hiking tour is sure to show you the best spots that you may overlook on your own. From summit hikes with stunning views to hiking trails alongside impressive waterfalls to paths lined with mountain wildflowers and favorite day hikes for families, there's an Asheville hike for almost everyone!
Cost: Free-$55 Age: T for teens and above (recommended)



Asheville Arboretum

While most major cities in the US have their own arboretums, Ashevilles' very own is a part of the Pisgah National forest. Spanning 65 acres, the arboretum features some of the US' largest azalea collection as well as many different themed gardens for almost anyone to enjoy. The North Carolina Arboretum's 434-acre campus is nestled off the Blue Ridge Parkway minutes from downtown Asheville and offers beautiful gardens and natural areas, including the Bonsai Exhibition Garden, 10 miles of forested hiking and biking trails, garden tours, nature activities for families, changing science, art and cultural history exhibits, an on-site bistro and gift shop.
Cost: $16 Age: E for Everyone



River Arts District

Located along the French Broad river, the river arts district is made up of older industrial buildings where local artists often display and produce their work. Souvenirs are often unique and are sold by these artists at fair prices. The graffiti and murals also make this a great location for photo ops. The River Arts District of Asheville consists of a vast array of artists and working studios set in an urban scene of old factories and historical buildings. Everywhere you turn, art abounds - with creativity happening around every corner. Many studios are open every day, all year round. Visitors are encouraged to come explore artist studios and watch while they work, or meet the artists and discuss the creative process.
Cost: free-$20 Age: E for Everyone
